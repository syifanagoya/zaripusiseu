Who is Expected to Win Globe Soccer Awards 2023?

It is highly likely that Cristiano Ronaldo will win a new award at the 2023 Globe Soccer Awards. Ronaldo is the clear favorite for the first-ever Best Middle East Player award, even though he will be up against fierce competition in the Best Men’s Player category, where Lionel Messi is also a contender.

Ronaldo is the clear favorite among the 12 nominees, which includes well-known athletes like Riyad Mahrez and Karim Benzema. This is due to his significant influence as the captain of Al Nassr. Although winning Men’s Player of the Year might present difficulties, Ronaldo is set to add the newly established Middle East Player award to his already impressive resume.

When is the Globe Soccer Awards 2023?

The 2023 Globe Soccer Awards will take place in Dubai on January 19, 2024. On Tuesday, the list of possible winners which included several FC Barcelona players was made public. The European Club Association, European Association of Player’s Agents, and Globe Soccer collaborated to organize the awards, which honor exceptional football accomplishments in several categories.

Important people from UEFA, FIFA, and international football clubs come together for the occasion, which promotes dialogue about the state of the sport today. Football fans look forward to the gala, where the winners will be announced and honored for their services to the game.

Globe Soccer Awards

The European Club Association (ECA) and the European Association of Player’s Agents (EFAA) collaborated to organize the football awards, which are presented by Globe Soccer. The League of the United Arab Emirates, major international clubs and their managers, the main representatives of the football world (FIFA, ECA, UEFA), and the football market are all present at the award ceremony, which takes place in Dubai, United Arab Emirates.

One of the main objectives of the ceremony is to foster a discussion about transfer-related matters and the football market.

Although the inaugural meeting took place in 2009, the first prizes three in total were not given out until 2010. The number of awards and categories was expanded to six in 2011. The 2012 version included ten football-related categories, which was a higher number. The award is sometimes referred to as the Dubai d’Or informally.
